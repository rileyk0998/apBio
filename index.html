Cell Cycle

It is a series of events that take place in a cell as it leads to the replication of DNA and the division of cytoplasm and organelles to produce two daughter cells.


Mitosis and Meiosis

Both are forms of eukaryotic cell division

Mitosis

Mitosis produces two daughter cells that are identical to the parent cell. During the S phase chromosomes replicated are divided so that each daughter cells receives a copy of every chromosome.

Meiosis

Meiosis produces four daughter cells. Those are either haploid sex cells or gametes (which contain a single copy of each chromosome) from diploid cells (which contain two copies of each chromosome).
