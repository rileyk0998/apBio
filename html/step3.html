Anaphase

Chromosomes seperate.

Anaphase is where chromosomes break at centromers. Sister chromatids move away from one another to opposite poles of the spindle. This is the shortest stage of mitosis.


Disjunction

The process in which the individual chromosomes are being pulled toward the poles by the kinetochore microtubules.


Nondisjunction

Nondisjunction is when disjunction fails because the chromosomes fail to seperate. This results in an uneven number of chromosomes on either end of the poles which results in mutations.

Telophase

Last phase and the seperation of the new cells.

This is the last stage of cell division and the cell undoes everything that was needed for cell division. The nuclear envelope is reformed. At this point, chromosomes move to opposite ends of the cell and two nuclei are formed.

This is where the cytoplasm divide creating two new cells.
