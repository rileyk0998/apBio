Prophase

Cells break down into parts to be reconfigured.

Chromosomes go from being in their chromatin form where they're all spread out to a more condensed form that you can actually see from a light microscope. This is all happening through chemical and thermodynamic reactions. The nuclear envelope starts to disappear. The centrosomes migrate to the opposite ends of the cell.


Spindle Apparatus (Mitotic Spindle)

When the chromosomes separate, that start to form something known as the spindle apparatus.


Microtubules Binding

Metaphase

Makes sure everything is properly set up.

This stage is where chromosomes become attached to the spindle fibers.


Checkpoints

The checkpoints make sure that the chromosomes are binded to the kinetochore microtubules correctly, so that the chromosomes divide equally.

By going through this procedure, it prevents the chance of mutation. If the checkpoints aren't met, mitosis will be put on hold until all the problems are fixed.
